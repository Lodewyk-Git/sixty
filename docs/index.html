The study received coverage across dozens of outlets. The headline: A person‚Äôs ability to be random peaks around 25 years old and declines after 60.

We initially set out to use the findings to make an age guessing game, but after our analysis we lost confidence that it would yield good results.

We don‚Äôt think the study‚Äôs findings are reproducible because of the researchers‚Äô decision to include questionable responses. To us, this meant the participant either misunderstood the instructions, or intentionally sabotaged the experiment.

The study was peer-reviewed, but to our knowledge it hasn‚Äôt been reproduced. This is typical, since reproduction takes a significant amount of time and effort on the part of other scientists busy conducting their own research. It is quite likely that the reviewers didn‚Äôt personally replicate the authors‚Äô data analysis either, which led the authors‚Äô decision to include the questionable responses to remain unnoticed.
